I am an Associate Professor in the Department of Philosophy at the University of Cincinnati. I'm also an affiliate of the History department, the Judaic Studies department, and the Center for Public Engagement with Science.

Most of my work is in historical philosophy of science. Other work includes philosophy of data, AI, and empirical research into loneliness. I'm a very amateur programmer, web designer (hopos.org and this site), wood-worker, and fan of non-intrusive home-automation.

Infrequently, I blog about academic tools. I am the Editor-in-Chief of the PhilSci-Archive.
